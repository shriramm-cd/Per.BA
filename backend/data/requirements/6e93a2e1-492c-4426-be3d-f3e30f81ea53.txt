2. Executive Summary
The organization currently lacks a unified loyalty and rewards program capable of consolidating customer
engagement across web, mobile, and in-store channels. Existing point-tracking mechanisms are
fragmented across three legacy systems, leading to inconsistent customer experiences, manual
reconciliation effort, and limited visibility into customer lifetime value.
This Business Requirements Document defines the business need, scope, and detailed requirements for a
new Customer Loyalty & Rewards Platform ("the Platform") that will replace existing systems with a single,
scalable solution. The Platform will enable real-time point accrual and redemption, tiered membership
benefits, personalized offers, and integration with the existing CRM and e-commerce systems.
The initiative is sponsored by the Customer Experience division and is expected to deliver measurable
improvements in customer retention, average order value, and program enrollment within twelve months of
launch. This document is intended for review and approval by key business and technical stakeholders
prior to the commencement of solution design.

6. Current State (As-Is) Analysis
The organization currently operates three disconnected loyalty mechanisms: an in-store punch-card style
system managed at the POS level, a web-based points system tied to the e-commerce platform, and a
manual spreadsheet-based process used by the call center to issue goodwill credits.
This fragmentation creates several pain points. Customers are unable to view a consolidated balance
across channels. Marketing teams cannot segment customers based on a unified lifetime value score.
Finance is unable to accurately forecast the loyalty liability on the balance sheet due to inconsistent point
valuation across systems. Additionally, customer support agents report an average of 45 minutes per week
per agent spent manually reconciling point discrepancies.

7. Future State (To-Be) Vision
In the future state, customers will enroll once and have their loyalty status and points balance reflected
consistently across web, mobile, and in-store channels in real time. A single source of truth for loyalty data
will allow marketing to build targeted campaigns based on tier, spend behavior, and engagement history.
Customers will be able to track progress toward the next membership tier, view personalized reward
recommendations, and redeem points instantly at checkout or in-store without delay. Internally, finance will
have access to a real-time dashboard reflecting outstanding point liability, and customer support will be
able to resolve loyalty-related inquiries within a single console rather than three.
The architecture will be built on a scalable, API-first foundation, allowing future expansion into co-branded
partnerships and additional redemption channels without significant rework.

9. Functional Requirements
9.1 Enrollment & Account Management
Customers shall be able to enroll in the loyalty program via web, mobile app, or in-store at the point of
sale.
The system shall prevent duplicate enrollments using email and phone number validation.
Customers shall be able to link multiple purchase channels to a single loyalty account.
9.2 Points Accrual & Redemption
The system shall calculate points earned based on configurable rules (e.g., 1 point per $1 spent).
Points shall be visible in the customer account within 5 minutes of transaction completion.
Customers shall be able to redeem points for discounts, free products, or experiential rewards.
The system shall support partial point redemption combined with other payment methods.
9.3 Tier Management
The system shall automatically upgrade or downgrade customer tier status based on rolling 12-month
spend.
Customers shall receive a notification when they move between tiers.
Each tier shall support a distinct set of configurable benefits managed by marketing administrators.
9.4 Administration & Reporting
Marketing administrators shall be able to create and schedule promotional point campaigns.
The system shall provide dashboards showing enrollment trends, redemption rates, and point liability.
All manual point adjustments shall be logged with user ID, timestamp, and reason code.

11. Assumptions, Constraints & Dependencies
11.1 Assumptions
Legacy system vendors will provide data export access for migration purposes.
Existing CRM and POS systems expose APIs suitable for real-time integration.
Marketing team will be available for workshops during the requirements refinement phase.
11.2 Constraints
The project must launch before the start of the next holiday shopping season.
Budget is capped at the amount approved in the Q3 capital expenditure plan.
Legacy POS hardware cannot be replaced as part of this initiative.
11.3 Dependencies
Completion of the CRM data model upgrade currently in progress under a separate initiative.
Availability of the integration team for POS middleware connectivity.
Legal and compliance sign-off on the updated loyalty program terms and conditions.
