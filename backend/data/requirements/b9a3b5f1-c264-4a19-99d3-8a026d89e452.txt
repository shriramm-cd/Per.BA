Executive SummaryThe organization currently lacks a unified loyalty and rewards program capable of consolidating customerengagement across web, mobile, and in-store channels. Existing point-tracking mechanisms arefragmented across three legacy systems, leading to inconsistent customer experiences, manualreconciliation effort, and limited visibility into customer lifetime value.This Business Requirements Document defines the business need, scope, and detailed requirements for anew Customer Loyalty & Rewards Platform ("the Platform") that will replace existing systems with a single,scalable solution. The Platform will enable real-time point accrual and redemption, tiered membershipbenefits, personalized offers, and integration with the existing CRM and e-commerce systems.The initiative is sponsored by the Customer Experience division and is expected to deliver measurableimprovements in customer retention, average order value, and program enrollment within twelve months oflaunch. This document is intended for review and approval by key business and technical stakeholdersprior to the commencement of solution design.3. Current State (As-Is) AnalysisThe organization currently operates three disconnected loyalty mechanisms: an in-store punch-card stylesystem managed at the POS level, a web-based points system tied to the e-commerce platform, and amanual spreadsheet-based process used by the call center to issue goodwill credits.This fragmentation creates several pain points. Customers are unable to view a consolidated balanceacross channels. Marketing teams cannot segment customers based on a unified lifetime value score.Finance is unable to accurately forecast the loyalty liability on the balance sheet due to inconsistent pointvaluation across systems. Additionally, customer support agents report an average of 45 minutes per weekper agent spent manually reconciling point discrepancies.Key Pain Points SummaryPain PointImpactNo unified customer viewPoor personalization, lost upsell opportunitiesManual reconciliationHigh operational cost, error-proneInconsistent point valuationInaccurate financial liability reportingNo real-time redemptionCustomer frustration, abandoned redemptions7. Future State (To-Be) VisionIn the future state, customers will enroll once and have their loyalty status and points balance reflectedconsistently across web, mobile, and in-store channels in real time. A single source of truth for loyalty datawill allow marketing to build targeted campaigns based on tier, spend behavior, and engagement history.Customers will be able to track progress toward the next membership tier, view personalized rewardrecommendations, and redeem points instantly at checkout or in-store without delay. Internally, finance willhave access to a real-time dashboard reflecting outstanding point liability, and customer support will beable to resolve loyalty-related inquiries within a single console rather than three.The architecture will be built on a scalable, API-first foundation, allowing future expansion into co-brandedpartnerships and additional redemption channels without significant rework.8. Functional Requirements9.1 Enrollment & Account ManagementCustomers shall be able to enroll in the loyalty program via web, mobile app, or in-store at the point ofsale.The system shall prevent duplicate enrollments using email and phone number validation.Customers shall be able to link multiple purchase channels to a single loyalty account.9.2 Points Accrual & RedemptionThe system shall calculate points earned based on configurable rules (e.g., 1 point per $1 spent).Points shall be visible in the customer account within 5 minutes of transaction completion.Customers shall be able to redeem points for discounts, free products, or experiential rewards.The system shall support partial point redemption combined with other payment methods.9.3 Tier ManagementThe system shall automatically upgrade or downgrade customer tier status based on rolling 12-monthspend.Customers shall receive a notification when they move between tiers.Each tier shall support a distinct set of configurable benefits managed by marketing administrators.9.4 Administration & ReportingMarketing administrators shall be able to create and schedule promotional point campaigns.The system shall provide dashboards showing enrollment trends, redemption rates, and point liability.All manual point adjustments shall be logged with user ID, timestamp, and reason code.10.
